THE SCIENTIFIC REVOLUTION
1600 – 1730 · the world becomes an equation

Galileo Galilei

1564 – 1642 · Pisa → Florence · The Scientific Revolution
“The book of nature is written in the language of mathematics.”

A lutenist’s son who turned a Dutch spyglass to the sky and a water-clock on a rolling ball. The telescope made him famous and the Inquisition made him a prisoner, but his deeper act was quieter: insisting that the fall of a stone is not a quality to be debated but a law to be written — and that the writing is mathematics.

KEY IDEAS

Nature speaks mathematics. Roll a ball down a ramp and the distances grow as the odd numbers — 1, 3, 5, 7 — the square law hiding inside plain falling. His claim that the physical world obeys number at bottom is the founding bet of modern science; every equation of physics since is that bet paying off.
Galileo’s paradox. Every number has a square, so there are exactly as many squares as numbers — yet almost no numbers are squares. He concluded that infinite collections defy comparison. Cantor picked up the same pairing 250 years later and built the sizes of infinity from it.

THE VANISHING POINT

an idea · Florence, 1420s · perspective → projective geometry
SPARK How do you paint depth onto a flat wall? In the 1420s Brunelleschi stood at the door of Florence’s cathedral with a painted panel and a mirror and showed there is an exact geometric answer — every set of parallel edges in the world aims at one point in the picture.
MOVE Take Euclid’s plane and add the points where parallels meet. Every family of parallel lines now shares one point at infinity, and the horizon is simply the line that collects them. Two centuries later Desargues turned the painters’ device into a geometry of its own — projective geometry, where there are no parallels at all.
WORLD Parallel lines meeting was the first crack in the belief that Euclid’s geometry is the geometry rather than a geometry — a crack that widens into curved space. And the discovery runs on your own hardware: stand between rails and watch them touch at the horizon. You are watching your visual system do projective geometry in real time. Painters simply wrote it down first.
